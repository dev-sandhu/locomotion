Cure for UTI

Probiotics are a form of bacterial interference and recommended as a treatment and prophylaxis strategy in women. Probiotics restore Lactobacillus sp vaginal flora and displace potential uropathogens from the vagina. Although fungi and viruses are also potential causes of UTI, bacteria are the most frequent. The gold criteria for diagnosing a bacterial UTI are urine culture and sensitivity. To inform the diagnostic and treatment approach, it is crucial to pinpoint the infection's site (such as the bladder, kidney, or prostate) as well as any comorbidities (such as diabetes mellitus or immunosuppression). The majority of treatment for a bacterial UTI is an antimicrobial drug, which is ideally chosen based on culture and sensitivity. Bacterial interference is the employment of low-virulence, nonpathogenic bacteria to outcompete more pathogenic species and reduce the likelihood of colonization and infection. E coli (strains 83972 and HU2117) and Lactobacillus sp. are two often used bacteria. The formation of bactericidin (an antibiotic protein), the competition for nutrition and attachment sites, the inhibition of biofilms, and host immunomodulation are some of the proposed mechanisms of action.



Reference: Olin, Shelly J, and Joseph W Bartges. “Urinary tract infections: treatment/comparative therapeutics.” The Veterinary clinics of North America. Small animal practice vol. 45,4 (2015): 721-46. doi:10.1016/j.cvsm.2015.02.005
