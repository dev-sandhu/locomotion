Microbiology

In the human mouth, gastrointestinal tract, and female genital tract, where they create lactic acid (leading in a low vaginal pH) and competitively inhibit pathogenic organisms, Lactobacillus species are typical flora. Dental caries is linked to Lactobacillus species. Some Lactobacillus species are frequently used as probiotics. Lactobacilli are gram-positive, nonmotile, tiny, slender bacilli that are found in chains. Young colonies have a smooth, convex, and translucent appearance. Organisms hydrolyze esculin and ferment carbohydrates; they are microaerophilic or anaerobic, and oxidase and catalase negative. Using the majority of commercial identification technologies, it can be challenging to correctly identify Lactobacillus. Improved microbial identification is possible thanks to the PCR assay, 16S rRNA gene sequencing, and MALDI-TOF mass spectrometry.





Reference: F. Bratcher, Denise. “Principles and Practice of Pediatric Infectious Diseases.” Other Gram-Positive Bacilli, Fifth Edition, 2016, pp. 133, https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-0-323-40181-4.00133-X.

UTI Infection

UTIs (urinary tract infections) continue to cause a lot of discomfort for women and are expensive for the healthcare system. Interest in non-antibiotic prevention of recurrent UTIs has increased as a result of rising antimicrobial resistance. Evidence shows that the microorganisms inhabit many sites of the body, including the urinary tract which has long been assumed to be sterile in healthy individuals, might have a role in maintaining urinary health. The most typical bacterial infection in polyclinic and service patients is urinary tract infection (UTI).

Caused by: Lactobacillus delbrueckii

Akgül, Turgay, and Tolga Karakan. “The role of probiotics in women with recurrent urinary tract infections.” Turkish journal of urology vol. 44,5 (2018): 377-383. doi:10.5152/tud.2018.48742

Diarrhea

One of the most common adverse effects cancer patients experience is diarrhoea. According to the literature, the incidence of chemotherapy (CT)-induced diarrhea (grade 3-4) in patients receiving treatment ranges from 10-20%, especially following the administration of a 5-fluorouracil (5-FU) bolus or certain combinations of irinotecan and fluoropyrimidines. The current study's objective was to assess the therapeutic efficacy of Lactobacillus kefiri LKF01 (Kefibios®) in the treatment or prevention of CT-related diarrhea in the cancer population.

Caused by: Lactobacillus acidophilus

Ghidini, Michele et al. “Lactobacillus Kefiri LKF01 (Kefibios®) for Prevention of Diarrhoea in Cancer Patients Treated with Chemotherapy: A Prospective Study.” Nutrients vol. 13,2 385. 27 Jan. 2021, doi:10.3390/nu13020385

Systemic Lupus Erythematosus

Despite the fact that it is generally recognized that a complex interaction between genes and environment is necessary for illness development, the cause of Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) is mostly unclear. Microbiota play an important role in immunological homeostasis as activators. It is believed that lactobacillus is an environmental factor that influences the onset of SLE. However, Lactobacillus' favorable therapeutic and anti-inflammatory benefits on SLE were also investigated as it is a major concern to be dealt with.

Caused by: Commensal Lactobacillus strain

Wang, Weijie et al. “Lactobacillus: Friend or Foe for Systemic Lupus Erythematosus?.” Frontiers in immunology vol. 13 883747. 23 May. 2022, doi:10.3389/fimmu.2022.883747
